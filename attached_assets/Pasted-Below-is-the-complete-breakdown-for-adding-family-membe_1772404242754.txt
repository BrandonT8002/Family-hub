FAMILYHUB — ADDING FAMILY MEMBERS (Full System Breakdown)

Core Philosophy

Adding a member should:

• Be structured
• Be age-aware
• Be permission-based
• Respect autonomy
• Protect minors
• Prevent loopholes

The system must balance:

Family unity
Individual privacy
Parental responsibility
Teen independence

⸻

STEP 1 — Owner Invites Member

Owner goes to:

Settings → Members → Add Member

Options:

• Add Adult (18+)
• Add Teen (15–17)
• Add Youth (13–14)
• Add Child (12 and under)
• Add Caregiver (separate system)

The age selection determines permissions automatically.

⸻

AGE-BASED STRUCTURE

⸻

1. Adult (18+)

Requirements

• Full name
• Date of birth
• Email required
• Password created by user

Permissions

• Full personal privacy
• Private diary
• Private schedule
• Private goals
• Private financial entries
• Full chat access
• Can leave family and create their own account

Owner Cannot:

• View adult private diary
• View adult private chats
• View adult private financial entries
• See who they are chatting with

Adults are autonomous.

⸻

2. Teen (15–17)

Designed as transitional adulthood.

Requirements

• Name
• Date of birth
• Email required (owner approves at invite stage)

Default Permissions

• Personal schedule
• Private diary
• Private chat
• Can create goals
• Can choose what to share

Owner Controls

• Financial visibility
• Outside-family communication
• Media sharing settings
• Shared schedule editing

Owner cannot:
• Automatically read diary
• Automatically see private schedule
• Automatically see private chats

If diary access requested:
Teen must approve entry-by-entry.

⸻

3. Youth (13–14)

More structured than teens.

Requirements

• Name
• Date of birth
• Email optional but encouraged
• Owner approval required for email integration

Permissions

• Personal schedule
• Personal goals
• Chat with approval
• Diary (private by default)

Owner controls:
• Financial access
• Media sharing
• Outside-family messaging
• Schedule sharing

At 13:
Prompt appears to integrate email (with owner approval).

⸻

4. Child (12 and Under)

Simplified onboarding.

Requirements

• Name
• Date of birth
• No email required
• Username + password created by owner

Permissions

• Limited schedule
• Limited goals
• Limited chat
• No outside family messaging
• No financial visibility

Notifications:
• Allowed only if owner permits
• No engagement-based nudges

If deleted:
• Data retained 1 year (if subscription active)
• Re-add possible during retention window

⸻

FAMILY TIERS (Capacity Rules)

The owner selects tier during signup.

⸻

Family Core

Up to 2 members
1 caregiver slot

⸻

Family Plus

Up to 5 members
2 caregiver slots

⸻

Family Extended

Up to 10 members
4 caregiver slots

⸻

Important Rules:

• Members cannot exceed tier capacity
• If at capacity, owner must remove member fully before adding new one
• Removed member has 24-hour transfer window
• After 24 hours → removed permanently from system

If owner upgrades tier:
Capacity expands immediately.

If owner downgrades:
Must remove members before downgrade takes effect.

⸻

CAREGIVER ADDITION (Separate from Family Member)

Caregivers:

• Do not count as full family member
• Limited access
• Assigned to specific child
• Cannot view finances
• Cannot view diary
• Cannot view private chats
• Cannot alter permissions

Caregiver slots are tier-dependent.

⸻

OUTSIDE FAMILY CONNECTIONS

Families can connect with other families.

This is NOT merging households.

⸻

How It Works

Owner → Connect to Another Family → Send Request

Both owners must approve.

Once connected:

They can:

• Share specific wishlists
• Coordinate gifts
• Schedule exchanges
• Chat between families

They cannot:

• See each other’s finances
• See goals
• See private schedule
• See diary
• See member permissions

Connections are limited and must be mutually accepted.

Owner can revoke connection anytime.

⸻

MEMBER REMOVAL RULES

If owner removes member:

• 24-hour file transfer window
• After 24 hours → removed completely
• No residual access
• No hidden association

If member leaves voluntarily:

• They can create their own family account
• Their personal data stays with them
• Shared data remains with family

If owner deletes account:

• Entire household enters grace period
• Data retained 1 year
• Requires re-subscription to restore

⸻

PERMISSION SUMMARY MATRIX

Adults:
Full privacy

15–17:
High autonomy, limited financial control

13–14:
Moderate autonomy, owner-controlled sharing

12 & Under:
Owner-controlled environment

Caregiver:
Assigned, limited, temporary

Outside Family:
Shared by permission only

⸻

WHY THIS STRUCTURE MATTERS

It:

• Protects minors
• Respects adults
• Encourages teen growth
• Prevents surveillance culture
• Prevents loopholes
• Keeps tiers meaningful
• Prevents account sharing abuse
