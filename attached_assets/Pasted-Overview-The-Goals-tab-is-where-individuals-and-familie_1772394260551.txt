Overview

The Goals tab is where individuals and families set intentions, track progress, and build momentum over time.

Goals are not pre-made by the app.
The app offers examples for quick setup, but the goal types are created by the owner/household to match the family’s real life.

Goals support:
	•	Short-term wins
	•	Long-term milestones
	•	Shared family objectives
	•	Personal self-improvement
	•	Money-linked saving goals
	•	Habit consistency tracking

The Goals tab is designed to feel motivating but never pushy.

⸻

Core Structure

1. Goal Categories (User-Created)

The owner can create custom goal categories, such as:
	•	Health
	•	Education
	•	Family
	•	Finance
	•	Home improvement
	•	Personal development
	•	Relationship
	•	Spiritual / mental wellness (optional)

During onboarding, the app can suggest common categories as “starter packs” that the user can accept, edit, or delete.

Users are never forced into a preset system.

⸻

2. Goal Types

A) Short-Term Goals

Designed for:
	•	1 day to 8 weeks
	•	Clear “finish line”
	•	Fast motivation

Examples:
	•	“Finish school supply list”
	•	“Clean garage by Saturday”
	•	“Save $100 this month”
	•	“Work out 3 times this week”

Short-term goals emphasize completion and momentum.

⸻

B) Long-Term Goals

Designed for:
	•	2 months to multi-year
	•	Big milestones
	•	Gradual progress

Examples:
	•	“Save for a couch”
	•	“Family vacation next year”
	•	“Pay off credit card”
	•	“Improve grades this semester”
	•	“Build emergency fund”

Long-term goals emphasize consistency and measurable progress.

⸻

3. Goal Ownership & Visibility

Each goal can be:
	•	Personal (visible only to creator)
	•	Shared (visible to selected family members)
	•	Family goal (visible to all members)

Teens can keep goals private unless they choose to share.

Children (12 and under) have visibility controlled by the owner.

Outside families cannot see goals. No cross-family goal visibility.

⸻

Progress Tracking

4. Progress Methods (Selectable per Goal)

Each goal can use one of these progress formats:

A) Checklist Progress

Best for:
	•	Shopping goals
	•	Projects
	•	School preparation

Example:
	•	✅ Buy notebooks
	•	✅ Get pencils
	•	⬜ Get glue

⸻

B) Numeric Progress

Best for:
	•	Savings
	•	Weight
	•	Reading pages
	•	Homework count

Example:
	•	“Save $1500” → $350 / $1500

⸻

C) Streak / Consistency Progress

Best for habits:
	•	Workouts
	•	Chores
	•	Studying
	•	Routine behaviors

Tracks:
	•	Days completed
	•	Missed days
	•	Best streak
	•	Weekly consistency

Rewards consistency (lightly), without addictive pressure.

⸻

D) Milestone Progress

Best for long-term plans:
	•	“Vacation planning”
	•	“Home renovation”
	•	“Semester grades”

Milestones can be added by the user.

Example:
	•	Research options ✅
	•	Book hotel ⬜
	•	Set budget ⬜

⸻

5. Progress Visualization

Goals show progress through:
	•	Progress bar
	•	Percent complete (optional)
	•	Weekly summary indicator
	•	Monthly improvement snapshot
	•	“Momentum” indicator (soft motivational feedback)

The tone is encouraging, never shaming.

⸻

Money-Linked Goals (Important)

6. Savings Goals Integration

Long-term goals can be linked to money:

Example:
“Couch Fund – $1500”
	•	Current saved: $350
	•	Family contributions allowed
	•	Tracks contributions over time
	•	Shows how much remains

This ties into Money tab charts and spending awareness.

⸻

Routine / Care Logs Integration (Optional)

7. Goals Can Tie Into Routines

Goals can optionally connect to:
	•	Workout routine tracker
	•	Chore reminders
	•	Baby/special needs care logs (feeding/diaper tracking)

Example:
“Baby feeding consistency”
	•	Logs tied to care chart

⸻

Goal Creation Flow (UX)

8. Creating a Goal

When user taps “New Goal”:

They choose:
	1.	Goal name
	2.	Short-term or long-term
	3.	Category (existing or new)
	4.	Visibility (personal vs shared)
	5.	Progress type (checklist / numeric / streak / milestone)
	6.	Optional due date
	7.	Optional reminders (gentle)

Then it appears on the Goals dashboard.

⸻

Goals Dashboard

9. Dashboard Views

Goals tab includes:
	•	“Active Goals” (default)
	•	“Shared Goals”
	•	“Completed Goals”
	•	“Archived Goals”
	•	Sorting options:
	•	Due soon
	•	Most progressed
	•	Least progressed
	•	Recently updated

Optional quick filter:
	•	Short-term only
	•	Long-term only

⸻

Retention Features (Long-Term Stickiness)

10. Goal Snapshots

The app can generate:
	•	Weekly goal progress snapshot
	•	Monthly “wins” summary
	•	Yearly goals reflection (completed vs archived)

This supports long-term retention and life-change messaging.

⸻

Permissions Summary
	•	Owner can set visibility defaults for minors
	•	Adults always have private goal option
	•	Teens (15–17) have autonomy
	•	No one outside the family can access goals

⸻

Emotional Purpose

The Goals tab should help families:
	•	Communicate what matters
	•	Build wealth and savings
	•	Stay aligned without nagging
	•	Celebrate progress
	•	Create shared meaning

This is where “this app changed our lives” becomes real.
